Our Story

Home, Healing, Hope

Esther was a queen who did things outside her comfort zone and saved her people. We use that story to help every woman who walks through our door understand something true about herself — that she is brave, beautiful, and capable of more than she knows.

"Our mission is to provide safe, supportive, faith-based housing for single, pregnant and parenting women — so they can focus on building stable, self-sustaining lives."

We are not an emergency shelter. We are a one-year program for women who are ready to invest in their future. We walk with them every step of the way.

What We Offer

Comprehensive support for
a whole season of life.

Every resident at Esther's Place receives far more than a roof. We walk with her through every dimension of her life — practical, emotional, educational, and spiritual.

🩺

Prenatal Care

We help every resident establish care with an OB/GYN or midwife and keep every routine medical appointment throughout her pregnancy.

🎓

Education & GED

Residents without a diploma attend C-Tec classes to pursue their GED or STNA credential — a foundation for lasting financial independence.

💼

Employment & Budgeting

Working-age residents build job skills and save 50% of their income during their stay — building real financial reserves before they leave.

👶

Parenting Skills

Childbirth preparation, newborn care, and parenting classes through Heartbeats Licking County and Licking Memorial Hospital's First Impressions program.

💬

Counseling & Case Management

Weekly meetings with our House Manager, plus individual counseling for emotional support, goal-setting, and connection to community resources.

✝️

Spiritual Foundation

Daily devotions, weekly worship services, and Embrace Grace Bible study give residents an anchor during a transformative season of their lives.

🌱

Life Skills Training

Practical coursework in budgeting, nutrition, meal planning, and infant development. Gratitude journaling. A reproducible workbook residents take with them when they leave.

🤝

Mentor Relationships

Approved mentors meet weekly or bi-weekly with residents — serving as role models, advisors, and friends throughout the program and beyond.

🌟

Aftercare Program

Support doesn't end at graduation. We offer ongoing aftercare for women as they move into the next season of their lives and navigate independence.

Ways to Volunteer

🙏 Community or Church Sponsor

Partner through financial support, events, or fundraisers. Sponsor a resident. Host a luncheon for your church or small group.

No background check required

🤝 Mentor

Meet weekly or every two weeks with a resident as a role model, advisor, and friend. One of the most meaningful things you can do.

Full vetting required

🚗 Driver or Babysitter

Drive residents to weekly classes at C-Tec or Heartbeats, or babysit for 2–3 hours during class time.

Background check required

🔨 Handyman

Indoor and outdoor maintenance at the home. Church men's groups especially welcome. All visits are supervised.

Paperwork required · Supervised

🍽️ Host a Luncheon

Pick a date, invite 4–6 guests, and let us tell the story. At your home, a restaurant, or right here at the maternity home. You provide the food and the people — we'll handle the rest.

This could be your home, too.

Esther's Place is a voluntary one-year program for single pregnant women ready to grow, heal, and build a stable future.

To be considered, you must:

  • ✓Be 18–25 years of age and single (unmarried, divorced, or separated)
  • ✓Be pregnant and in your first or second trimester
  • ✓Not currently fleeing a domestic violence situation
  • ✓Be drug-free with at least 6 months of sobriety
  • ✓Be committed to the program's structure and expectations
  • ✓Pass a background check — no assault or disorderly conduct

We are not an emergency shelter. Our program is designed for women who are ready to invest in building a different future. If that's you — we want to hear from you.

📞 Call us: 614-226-0864

How the process works
1

Submit your inquiry

Fill out the form below. We'll review your information and reach out within 48 hours.

2

Phone screening

A brief call to review the criteria and answer your questions about the program and what daily life looks like.

3

In-person or video interview

Meet with our House Manager to talk about your story, your goals, and what you're hoping for.

4

Acceptance decision

The full process — inquiry to move-in — typically takes 7–10 days.
